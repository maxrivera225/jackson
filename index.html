Jackson Heights: I.S. 145 School-Based Health Center Suddenly Closes

The clinic closure is part of a growing trend of school-based health centers shuttered in the last two years.

Listen to an audio reporting version of this story here. A version of this story was published by the Queens Daily Eagle.

By Max Rivera

November 29, 2023

QUEENS, NY –– Of the eight Gotham Health clinics closed in August, the closing of the clinic inside I.S. 145 hit their students the hardest, according to a petition organized by teachers and parents to save the clinic.

School administrators who rely solely on published ratios to determine adequate staffing levels of school nurses overlook the most important part of the National Association of School Nurses’ guidance, Kate King, the association’s president said.

“The ratio is one nurse for every 750 healthy students,” she said, emphasizing that higher risk student populations, including the uninsured, need more school nurses and school-based health centers.

School-based health care is the frontline of defense against chronic absenteeism.

King said the number one cause of chronic absenteeism is asthma.
